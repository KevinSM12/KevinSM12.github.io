Uses for Minecraft in an Education Setting

Minecraft is an open world sandbox exploration game where you can do basically whatever you want.
Thanks to this freedom many people have used the game as a medium to demonstrate real world
scientific phenomena such as gravity and sedimentary rock. Due to these efforts by many inteligent people
Mojang the creator of minecraft developed an official Education Edition of Minecraft for use in schools
to help lessons feel more engaging. They added all new features like a periodic table of elements.

These are some of the new features in this edition

  • Classroom Mode
  • Camera
  • Code Connection
  • Lab Table
  • Underwater TNT
  • Hardened Glass
  • Colored Torches
  • Balloons

Some of these features are here to help teachers have a greater control over the lesson, such as
Classroom Mode. This is a separate copatiable app that allows the teacher to see a top down view of the world
and a list of all the players. Also they can give the students items or teleport them to help them stay on track.
Most of the other features are here to educate the students on a wide range of topics. From chemistry to physics
to coding can be taught in this edition.
